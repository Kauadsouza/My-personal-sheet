## The capital myth

Everyone believes you need money to build a product. An investor, expensive servers, a big team. The reality is different — especially in 2026, with the tools available.

When my co-founder and I decided to create The Kaden, we had two assets: time and technical skill.

## What actually matters in the first 90 days

In the first three months of a product, one thing matters: **validating that someone will pay for what you're building**.

Not perfect code. Not a beautiful UI. Not a scalable system.

The question is: will someone open their wallet for this?

## How we got there

**Week 1-2:** We interviewed 12 small business owners about WhatsApp customer service. We asked how much time they spent answering repetitive messages. The average was 3 hours per day.

**Week 3-4:** We built a functional prototype in 8 days. Ugly, but it worked.

**Week 5-8:** We presented to those 12 contacts. 4 wanted to use it immediately. 2 paid for early access.

That was the moment we knew we were building something real.

## The tools that cost us zero

- **Vercel**: free deployment for the dashboard
- **Supabase**: database on the free tier
- **WhatsApp Business API**: through partners with free initial access
- **Resend**: email sending (3,000/month free)

Total infrastructure cost in month one: **$0**.

## What I learned

1. **Validation before code**: Before writing a line, understand the problem.
2. **Speed beats perfection**: An ugly prototype delivered in 2 weeks is worth more than a beautiful product delivered in 6 months.
3. **The right co-founder**: Someone with complementary skills is worth more than any investment.
4. **Paying customers > free users**: The first dollar paid completely changes how you see the product.

If you have an idea and are waiting to have money to start, you're waiting for the wrong thing.
